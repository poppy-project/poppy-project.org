Jonathan Grizou

Jonathan Grizou is specialized in artificial intelligence and its application to human-robot interfaces. During his PhD under the supervision of Dr. Manuel Lopes and Dr. Pierre-Yves Oudeyer, Jonathan was interested in building smarter and more adaptive human-machine interfaces. The practical applications of his work to brain-computer interaction was awarded the "Prix Le Monde de la Recherche Universitaire 2015", a prize awarded to 5 young French scientists across all fields of science.

Jonathan is a long time maker with advanced abilities in both electronics, mechanics, and programming. He was using professional CAD software by age 12 and built his own 3D printer back in 2011. His practical knowledge of robotics, combined with his expert knowledge in artificial intelligence, provide a set of transversal skills primordial for robotics innovation.
